Definition

    • Local Area Network: collection of computers in a single building or building complex
    • Metropolitan Area Network: spans a local community
    • Wide Area Network: links devices in the next city or across the world

    • The machines are all connected to a common communication line called a bus
    • One machine serves as a central focal point to which all the others are connected

    • Carrier Sense, Multiple Access with Collision Detection: dictates that each message be broadcast to all the machines on the bus
    • Carrier Sense, Multiple Access with Collision Avoidance: tries to avoid collisions within the bus

    • Passes signals back and forth between the two original buses without considering the meaning of the signals
    • Forwards a message across the connection only when that message is destined for a computer on the other side. Thus, two machines residing on the same side of a bridge can exchange messages without interfering with communication taking place on the other side
    • Produces a network consisting of several buses extending from the switch, as spokes on a wheel
    • Recieves a message from the AP and forwards the message to the router at the Ethernet
    • The “point” at which one network is linked to an internet

    • Processes that provide service to and receive service from each other
    • Consist of software units that execute as processes on different computers
    • Huge pools of shared computers on the network can be allocated for use by clients as needed

    • At the top of the hierarchy and consist of very high-speed, high-capacity, international WANs
    • Tend to be more regional in scope and less potent in their capabilities
    • An intermediary between devices and the "core of the Internet"

    • Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers: a nonprofit established to coordinate the Internet
    • Domain Name System: converts mnemonic adresses to IP adresses
    • Suffixes used to denote the domain's classification, such as .com, .org, .edu
    • Two-letter TLDs for each specific country, ex: .au, .ca

    • Transfer of files from one host to another over a TCP-based network, such as the Internet
    • Loads webpages using hypertext links
    • Defines a way that two computers on the network may interact when transmitting an email message from one host to the other

    • Software that infects a computer by inserting itself into programs that already reside in the machine
